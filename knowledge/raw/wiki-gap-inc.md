The Gap, Inc.

**The Gap, Inc.**,[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-4) commonly known as **Gap Inc.**, is an American multinational clothing and accessories retailer. Gap was founded in 1969 by [Donald Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Fisher "Donald Fisher") and [Doris F. Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doris_F._Fisher "Doris F. Fisher") and is headquartered in San Francisco, California. The company operates four primary divisions: the namesake Gap, [Banana Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banana_Republic "Banana Republic"), [Old Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Navy "Old Navy"), and [Athleta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athleta_(fashion) "Athleta (fashion)"). Gap Inc. is one of the largest apparel retailers in the United States, and is third in total international locations, behind [Inditex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inditex "Inditex") Group and [H&M](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%26M "H&M").[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-5) As of early 2023, Gap employs about 95,000 people.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-6)

The Fisher family remains deeply involved in the company, collectively owning much of its stock.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-7) [Donald Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Fisher "Donald Fisher") was [chairman of the board](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chairman_of_the_board "Chairman of the board") until 2004, playing a role in the ouster of then-CEO [Millard Drexler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millard_Drexler "Millard Drexler") in 2002, and remained on the board until his death in 2009. Fisher's wife and their son, [Robert J. "Bob" Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_J._Fisher "Robert J. Fisher"), are also on Gap's board of directors. Bob succeeded his father as chairman in 2004 and was [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_Executive_Officer "Chief Executive Officer") on an interim basis following the resignation of [Paul Pressler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Pressler_(businessman) "Paul Pressler (businessman)") in 2007, before being succeeded by Glenn K. Murphy up until 2014. From February 2015 to November 2019, [Art Peck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_Peck "Art Peck") was CEO of Gap Inc.,[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-8) until he was replaced by [Sonia Syngal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonia_Syngal "Sonia Syngal") in March 2020.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-9) Syngal stepped down in July 2022, with executive chairman Bob Martin as interim CEO.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-10) In February 2024, Gap appointed American fashion designer [Zac Posen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zac_Posen "Zac Posen") as Creative Director of Gap, and Chief Creative Officer for Old Navy.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-11)

## History

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

In 1969, Don Fisher, a California commercial real estate broker specializing in retail store location, enlisted the help of his friend, [Walter Haas Jr.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Haas_Jr. "Walter Haas Jr."), President of [Levi Strauss & Co.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levi_Strauss_%26_Co. "Levi Strauss & Co.") Fisher was inspired by the sudden success of 'The Tower of Shoes' in an old [Quonset hut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quonset_hut "Quonset hut") in a non-retail industrial area of [Sacramento, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California "Sacramento, California"),[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-AMAZON-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-The-13) that drew crowds by advertising that no matter what brand, style or size of shoes a woman could want it was at The Tower of Shoes. And knowing that even [Macy's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macy%27s "Macy's"), the biggest Levi's customer, was constantly running out of the best selling Levi's sizes and colors, Fisher asked Haas to let him copy The Tower of Shoes' business model and apply it to Levi's products. Haas referred Fisher to Bud Robinson, his Director of Advertising, for what Haas assumed would be a quick refusal; but instead Robinson and Fisher carefully worked out a legal test plan for what was to become the Gap (named by Don's wife Doris Fisher). The name was a reference to the " [generation gap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_gap "Generation gap")".[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-14)

Fisher agreed to stock only [Levi's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levi_Strauss_%26_Co. "Levi Strauss & Co.") apparel in every style and size, all grouped by size, and Levi's guaranteed the Gap to be never out of stock by overnight replenishment from Levi's [San Jose, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Jose,_California "San Jose, California") warehouse. And finally, Robinson offered to pay 50% of the Gap's radio advertising upfront and avoided antitrust laws by offering the same marketing package to any store that agreed to sell nothing but Levi's products.

Fisher opened the first Gap store near [City College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City_College_of_San_Francisco "City College of San Francisco") on Ocean Avenue in [Ingleside, San Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ingleside,_San_Francisco "Ingleside, San Francisco") on August 21, 1969; its only merchandise consisted of Levi's and [LP records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LP_record "LP record") to attract teen customers.

In 1970, Gap opened its second store in San Jose. In 1971, Gap established its corporate headquarters in [Burlingame, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burlingame,_California "Burlingame, California") with four employees. By 1973, the company had over 25 locations and had expanded into the East Coast market with a store in the Echelon Mall in [Voorhees, New Jersey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voorhees,_New_Jersey "Voorhees, New Jersey"). In 1974, Gap began to sell [private label merchandise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Private_label "Private label").[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-15)

In the 1990s, Gap assumed an upscale identity and revamped its inventory under the direction of [Mickey Drexler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mickey_Drexler "Mickey Drexler").[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-16) However, Drexler was removed from his position after 19 years in 2002 after over-expansion, a 29-month slump in sales, and tensions with the Fisher family. Drexler refused to sign a non-compete agreement and eventually became CEO of [J. Crew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Crew "J. Crew"). One month after his departure, merchandise that he had ordered was responsible for a strong rebound in sales.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-17)[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-18)[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-19) [Robert J. Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_J._Fisher "Robert J. Fisher") recruited [Paul Pressler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Pressler_(businessman) "Paul Pressler (businessman)") as the new CEO; he was credited with closing under-performing locations and paying off debt. However, his focus groups failed to recover the company's leadership in its market.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/GAP_Logo.svg/250px-GAP_Logo.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:GAP_Logo.svg) Brand logo, which is a revision of 1988 wordmark, used since 2017

In 2007, Gap announced that it would "focus \[its\] efforts on recruiting a chief executive officer who has deep retailing and merchandising experience ideally in apparel, understands the creative process and can effectively execute strategies in large, complex environments while maintaining strong financial discipline". That January, Pressler resigned after two disappointing holiday sales seasons and was succeeded by Robert J. Fisher on an interim basis.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-20)[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-21)[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-22) He began working with the company in 1980 and joined the board in 1990, and would later assume several senior executive positions, including president of Banana Republic and the Gap units.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-23) The board's search committee was led by Adrian Bellamy, chairman of [The Body Shop International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Body_Shop "The Body Shop") and included founder Donald Fisher. On February 2, Marka Hansen, the former head of the Banana Republic division, replaced [Cynthia Harriss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cynthia_Harriss "Cynthia Harriss") as the leader of the Gap division. The executive president for marketing and merchandising Jack Calhoun became interim president of Banana Republic.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-24) In May, Old Navy laid off approximately 300 managers in lower volume locations to help streamline costs. That July, Glenn Murphy, previously CEO of [Shoppers Drug Mart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shoppers_Drug_Mart "Shoppers Drug Mart") in Canada, was announced as the new CEO of Gap, Inc. New lead designers were also brought on board to help define a fashionable image, including [Patrick Robinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Robinson_(fashion_designer) "Patrick Robinson (fashion designer)") for Gap Adult, Simon Kneen for Banana Republic, and [Todd Oldham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Oldham "Todd Oldham") for Old Navy. Robinson was hired as chief designer in 2007, but was dismissed in May 2011 after sales failed to increase. However, he enjoyed commercial success in international markets.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-25)[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-26)[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-27)

In October 2011, Gap Inc. announced plans to close 189 US stores, nearly 21 percent, by the end of 2013; however, it also plans to expand its presence in China.[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-28)[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-29) The company announced it would open its first stores in Brazil in the Fall of 2013.[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-30)

In January 2015, Gap Inc. announced plans to close its subsidiary [Piperlime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piperlime "Piperlime") in order to focus on its core brands. The first and only Piperlime store, based in [SoHo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SoHo "SoHo"), New York City, closed in April.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-31)

In September 2018, Gap Inc. began publicizing Hill City, a men's athletic apparel brand that launched in October 2018.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-32)

In June 2020, Gap Inc. announced that it had made the decision to wind down its Hill City brand in the coming months.[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-33) Weeks later, the company announced its collaboration with [Kanye West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanye_West "Kanye West")'s [Yeezy Brand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adidas_Yeezy "Adidas Yeezy"): [Yeezy Gap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeezy_Gap "Yeezy Gap"). The announcement of the company's 10-year long contract with the music icon turned fashion innovator saw Gap Inc. garner $34.9 million in media impact value.

In August 2020, the company announced that it, alongside its Banana Republic brand, would close over 225 store locations as a result of the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic") response.[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-34) Less than two months later, the company announced that the total number of stores to be closed by 2024 was 350. (220 Gap stores and 130 Banana Republic stores.)[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-35) The original plan of the company was to close only 90 stores, however, it expanded the number as a consequence of the financial effects caused by the pandemic restrictions. Most of the stores closed were ones set in malls.

In November 2020, Gap Inc. partnered with [Afterpay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afterpay "Afterpay"). This collaboration was planned to improve the digital shopping experience.[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-36)[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-37)

In February 2021, Gap Inc. announced a $140 million investment to build an 850,000 square foot distribution center in Longview, Texas, because it forecasts that its online business will double over the next two years. The new center will be able to process one million packages per day once completed in 2022.[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-38) In August 2021, Gap acquired Drapr, an online tech application where customers can create 3D Avatars and virtually try on clothing.[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-39)

In September 2021, Gap Inc. cut the ribbon for the $41.7 million facility expansion in Gallatin, TN. In addition to hiring 1,100 employees to meet the demands of market share growth and peak season, Gap partnered with [AHS](https://www.ahs1.com/) to implement an automated order fulfillment system.

In September 2022, the company announced it would end its partnership with Kanye West.[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-40) Several days later, Gap announced it would cut some 500 corporate positions in its San Francisco and New York offices.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-gap_layoffs-41) The firm also cut corporate jobs in Asia.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-42) Gap announced that the layoffs were unrelated to the decision to end its partnership with West.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-gap_layoffs-41) Gap announced more layoffs in April 2023.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-43)

Gap Inc. has decided to completely leave Russia, as well as close all stores in the country, due to the unpopularity of the brand in Russia.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-44)

In 2023, Gap launched a programme to support emerging designers in their early stages of development. The programme is sponsored by the company's internal initiative, Create With Audacity, providing space, tools and resources to develop talent. In partnership with ICON360, the non-profit arm of Harlem's Fashion Row Gap supports underrepresented Black designers in the industry, as well as fashion programmes at [Historically Black Colleges and Universities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historically_Black_Colleges_and_Universities "Historically Black Colleges and Universities") (HBCUs).[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-45)

In 2023, Gap partnered with [Cargill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cargill "Cargill"), [GSK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GSK_plc "GSK plc"), [WaterAid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WaterAid "WaterAid") and the Water Resilience Coalition to launch the Women + Water Collaborative to improve access to clean water and sanitation in India, starting in the [Krishna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krishna_River "Krishna River") and [Godavari River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godavari_River "Godavari River") basins. The Collaborative continues to build on the work of a previous initiative of USAID Gap Inc. Women + Water Alliance, which has empowered more than 2.4 million people to improve their access to water and sanitation in India between 2017 and 2023.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-46)

In May 2024, Gap introduced its limited collection in partnership with the Californian clothing brand, Dôen, celebrating sisterhood.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-47)

On August 22, 2024, Gap changed its ticker symbol from "GPS" to "GAP". The "GAP" ticker symbol was previously used by [A&P](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A%26P "A&P") until it filed for bankruptcy in 2010.

On September 4, 2025, Gap announced its intentions to be selling [personal care](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_care_products "Personal care products") and [cosmetics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmetics "Cosmetics") at its Old Navy stores however, it did not give a clear timeframe upon this will commence but will first test out this concept at 150 Old Navy locations.[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-48)

### Logo

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Logo")\]

Gap Inc. owns a [trademark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark "Trademark") to its name, "Gap". The Gap's original trademark was a [service mark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark "Service mark") for _retail clothing store services_. The application was filed with the [United States Patent and Trademark Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Patent_and_Trademark_Office "United States Patent and Trademark Office") on February 29, 1972, by the Gap Stores; registration was granted on October 10, 1972. The first use of the trademark was on August 23, 1969, and expanded to commercial usage on October 17, 1969. A second application was filed by Gap Stores, Inc. on September 12, 1970, this time for a trademark filed for [shirts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shirt "Shirt"). The first usage for shirts and clothing products was on June 25, 1974. Trademark registration was granted on December 28, 1976. Both the service mark and trademark are registered and owned by Gap (Apparel), LLC of San Francisco, California.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

On October 4, 2010, in an effort to establish a contemporary presence, Gap introduced a new logo. It was designed with the [Helvetica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helvetica "Helvetica") font and reduced the prominence of the brand's iconic blue box. After much public outcry, the company reverted to its previous "blue box" logo on October 11, after less than a week in use.[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-49)[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-50) Marka Hansen, the executive who oversaw the logo change, resigned February 1, 2011.[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-51)

### Brands

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Brands")\]

#### Banana Republic

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Banana Republic")\]

[Banana Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banana_Republic "Banana Republic"), a small safari-themed clothing retailer, was purchased by Gap in 1983 and was rebranded as an upscale clothing retailer in the late 1980s.

#### Old Navy

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Old Navy")\]

[Old Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Navy "Old Navy") was launched in 1994 as a value chain. On February 28, 2019, Gap Inc., announced that Old Navy will [spin-off](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporate_spin-off "Corporate spin-off") from the company, making Old Navy independent from Gap Inc.[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-52) This was reversed on January 16, 2020, when Gap Inc. announced that the separation had been called off.[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-53)

#### Athleta

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Athleta")\]

Athleta was founded in 1998 as an independent company focused on women's athletic apparel. Gap acquired Athleta in 2008.[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-54) Gap opened the first brick and mortar Athleta store in 2011.[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-55)

#### Forth & Towne

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Forth & Towne")\]

[Forth & Towne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forth_%26_Towne "Forth & Towne"), the company's fourth traditional retail concept, was launched on August 24, 2005, featuring apparel targeted toward women 35 years and older.[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-56) On February 26, 2007, after an 18-month trial period, it was discontinued.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/San_Jose_Gap_store.jpg/250px-San_Jose_Gap_store.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:San_Jose_Gap_store.jpg) A Gap location in [Westfield Valley Fair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westfield_Valley_Fair "Westfield Valley Fair"), [San Jose, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Jose,_California "San Jose, California")

The Gap targeted the younger generation when it opened, with its name referring to the [generation gap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_gap "Generation gap") of the time.[\[57\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-57) It originally sold everything that Levi Strauss & Co made in every style, size, and color, and organized the stock by size. The Gap was the first of many shops that carried only [Levi's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levi_Strauss_%26_Co. "Levi Strauss & Co."). In 1973, Gap started making its own jeans as a way to differentiate themselves from department stores.[\[58\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-58) Gap's marketing works to appeal to a broad demographic of customers, whereas [Banana Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banana_Republic "Banana Republic") presents a sophisticated image with a self expressing easygoing personality and [Old Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Navy "Old Navy") focuses "fun, fashion, and value" for families and younger customers. While the company has been criticized for blandness and uniformity in its selling environments, it maintains that it tailors its stores "to appeal to unique markets" by developing multiple formats and designs.[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-59) The domain _www.gap.com_ attracts over 18 million visitors annually, according to a 2008 [Compete.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compete.com "Compete.com") survey.[\[60\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-60) In 2018, a Gap ad campaign featuring a young girl wearing a [hijab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hijab "Hijab") stirred up controversy in France.[\[61\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-61)

### International presence

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: International presence")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/GapHillcrestMall3.jpg/250px-GapHillcrestMall3.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:GapHillcrestMall3.jpg) Gap in [Hillcrest Mall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hillcrest_Mall "Hillcrest Mall")[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Gap_store_in_the_Westfield_Sydney_closing_down_sale_December_2017.jpg/250px-Gap_store_in_the_Westfield_Sydney_closing_down_sale_December_2017.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Gap_store_in_the_Westfield_Sydney_closing_down_sale_December_2017.jpg) The closing down sale at the Gap store in [Westfield Sydney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westfield_Sydney "Westfield Sydney")

Including both company-owned and franchised stores, as of June 2018, there were Gap, [Banana Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banana_Republic "Banana Republic"), Athleta, Intermix, or Old Navy stores in 43 countries.[\[62\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-62) In January 2008, Gap signed a deal with Marinopoulos Group to open Gap and Banana Republic stores in Greece, Romania, Bulgaria, Cyprus, and Croatia.[\[63\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-63) In February 2009, Elbit Imaging, Ltd. secured a franchise to open and operate Gap and Banana Republic stores in Israel.[\[64\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-64) In August 2010, the company opened its first store in [Melbourne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melbourne "Melbourne"), Australia at [Chadstone Shopping Centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chadstone_Shopping_Centre "Chadstone Shopping Centre").[\[65\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-65)[\[66\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-66) In September 2011, Komax opened the first Gap store in Chile, due to a franchise.[\[67\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-67) In October 2011, the first GAP store opened in [Warsaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warsaw "Warsaw"), Poland.[\[68\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-68) Gap has a store in New Delhi, India which opened in May 2015. On February 20, 2016, Gap launched stores in Mumbai at Oberoi Mall and Infinity-2.

In May 2016, Gap Inc. announced it would shutter all Old Navy stores in Japan in response to poor Q1 performance for Old Navy and consistent losses across the organization.[\[69\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-69)

In 2017, Gap closed all seven of its stores in Israel.[\[70\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-70) In 2018, Gap closed all its stores in Australia.[\[71\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-71)

By May 2021, Gap operated company-owned stores in the United States, Canada, Mexico, the United Kingdom, France, India, Italy, the Czech Republic, Ireland, Japan, Philippines, China, and Taiwan.[\[72\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-72)

However, in June 2021, Gap confirmed plans to close all its 81 stores in the UK and Ireland and go online-only. The company said it would close all its stores "in a phased manner" between the end of August and the end of September. At the same time, Gap said it was in negotiations with another firm to take over all of its French stores. In Italy, Gap said it was in discussions with a partner for the potential acquisition of the stores there.[\[73\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-73) In September 2021, Gap and British clothes retailer [Next](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_plc "Next plc") announced a joint venture that will see Next manage Gap's UK website and place Gap concessions in some stores. The deal preserves Gap's presence on the UK high street following the closure of its own stores.[\[74\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-74) In November 2022, Baozun announced it intended to purchase Gap's China unit, and that it would continue to operate Gap stores in China and Taiwan as franchises.[\[75\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-75)

In 2006, Gap took part in the [Product Red](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Product_Red "Product Red") campaign with the launch of a special RED collection, including a T-shirt manufactured in [Lesotho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesotho "Lesotho") from African cotton. National Labor Committee for Worker and Human Rights activists criticized Gap's partnerships because Gap has historically been accused of sweatshop-like conditions.[\[77\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-77) Product Red has contributed over $45 million to the Global Fund, more than any other private donation received to date. In 1999, news outlets reported instances [sweatshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweatshop "Sweatshop") workers in [Saipan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saipan "Saipan") not being paid for overtime work, being subjected to forced abortion, and being required to work in unsafe working conditions.[\[78\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-78) In 2003, a [class action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Class_action "Class action") lawsuit against Gap and 21 other companies was started which ended when a [settlement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Settlement_(litigation) "Settlement (litigation)") of $20 million was reached.[\[79\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-79)

In May 2006, adult and child employees of Western Factory, a supplier in [Irbid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irbid "Irbid"), Jordan, were found to have worked up to 109 hours per week and to have gone six months without being paid. Some employees claimed they had been raped by managers.[\[80\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-NLCNET-80) The government of Jordan launched an investigation into the supplier and other textile factories and announced actions to prevent future abuses.[\[81\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-81) [Walmart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walmart "Walmart"), who also sources from the supplier, confirmed "serious problems with working conditions" at Western Factory and other Jordanian suppliers. As of May 2018[\[update\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gap_Inc.&action=edit), Gap had ceased its relationship with Western Factory.[\[82\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-82)

On October 28, 2007, [BBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC "BBC") footage showed [child labor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child_labor "Child labor") in Indian Gap factories.[\[83\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-83) The company denied knowledge of the happenings; it subsequently removed and destroyed the single piece of clothing in question, a smock blouse, from a British store. Gap promised to investigate breaches in its ethical policy.[\[84\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-84)

On February 19, 2014, Glenn Murphy, then CEO of Gap Inc., announced that Gap would raise the minimum wages for its 65,000 U.S. store employees.[\[85\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-Gap_Readies_Minimum_Wage_Increase-85)

In 2020, the [Australian Strategic Policy Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_Strategic_Policy_Institute "Australian Strategic Policy Institute") accused at least 82 major brands, including Gap Inc., of being connected to forced [Uyghur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs") labor in [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang").[\[86\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-86)

In 2020, a partnership between Gap and the rapper [Kanye West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanye_West "Kanye West") was announced as a part of a 10-year deal. However, West accused Gap of failing to honor terms of the deal and decided to end the partnership in September 2022.[\[87\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_Inc.#cite_note-87)
